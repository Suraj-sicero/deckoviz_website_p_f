# A Collection of Profound Literary and Intellectual Passages

<b>Sentences that bend reality</b>

Some passages do more than communicate ideas.

They rearrange the furniture of the mind.

A single paragraph can destabilize certainty, open a new dimension of thought, or linger for decades as a quiet internal companion.
These are not lines we simply read and move past. They return to us. In moments of stillness. In moments of crisis. In moments when the surface explanations of the world feel thin.

Across literature, philosophy, science, mathematics, and human reflection, certain passages have achieved a rare density.

They compress entire worldviews into a few sentences.

They do not argue.

They state.

And in doing so, they invite the reader to sit with discomfort, wonder, humility, or awe.
What makes a passage profound is not its complexity.

It is its ability to feel inexhaustible.

You can revisit it years later and discover a new meaning waiting for you.

The words did not change.

You did.
At Deckoviz, we see passages like these as cognitive landmarks.

They are not decorative quotes.

They are anchors.

They deserve space, time, and atmosphere.

A great passage should not be glanced at while scrolling.

It should be encountered.
With Deckoviz, passages can become living presences in a space.

A line can surface slowly in the morning light.

A paragraph can fade in and out across the day.

Words can be paired with abstract visuals, subtle motion, and sound that amplify their emotional and philosophical gravity.
This collection brings together passages that have shaped how humans think about existence, time, consciousness, reality, meaning, and the limits of knowledge.

They come from different eras and disciplines, but they share one trait.

They refuse to be forgotten.

## Foundational Passages on Existence, Time, and Reality

**“We are such stuff as dreams are made on, and our little life is rounded with a sleep.”**  
📖 **The Tempest   William Shakespeare (1611)**  
A meditation on the ephemeral nature of life, blending poetry with metaphysical depth.

<br>

**“The eternal silence of these infinite spaces terrifies me.”**  
📖 **Pensées   Blaise Pascal (1669)**  
A stark confrontation with the incomprehensible vastness of the universe.

<br>

**“What we call reality is in fact nothing more than a culturally sanctioned and linguistically reinforced hallucination.”**  
📖 **Terence McKenna**  
A challenge to perception, drawing from philosophy, neuroscience, and language.

<br>

**“Every inanimate object is just an animate object waiting for time to catch up.”**  
📖 **Carlo Rovelli   The Order of Time (2018)**  
A radical rethinking of time, motion, and stillness.

<br>

**“The fault, dear Brutus, is not in our stars, but in ourselves, that we are underlings.”**  
📖 **Julius Caesar   William Shakespeare (1599)**  
A timeless assertion of human agency over fate.

<br>

**“You are not a drop in the ocean. You are the entire ocean in a drop.”**  
📖 **Rumi (13th century)**  
A mystical insight into the boundless nature of the self.

<br>

**“The only way to deal with this life meaningfully is to find meaning in something that is greater than yourself.”**  
📖 **Viktor Frankl   Man’s Search for Meaning (1946)**  
A testament to the resilience of the human spirit.

<br>

**“It is not the strongest of the species that survive, nor the most intelligent, but the one most responsive to change.”**  
📖 **Attributed to Charles Darwin   On the Origin of Species (1859)**  
A fundamental truth of evolution and adaptation.

<br>

**“There is a crack in everything. That's how the light gets in.”**  
📖 **Leonard Cohen   Anthem (1992)**  
A poetic expression of imperfection as the gateway to wisdom.

<br>

**“If you want to make an apple pie from scratch, you must first invent the universe.”**  
📖 **Carl Sagan   Cosmos (1980)**  
A stunning encapsulation of the interconnectedness of all things.

<br>

**“The cosmos is within us. We are made of star-stuff. We are a way for the universe to know itself.”**  
📖 **Carl Sagan   Cosmos (1980)**  
A poetic and scientific reflection on our cosmic origins.

<br>

**“There is a single light of science, and to brighten it anywhere is to brighten it everywhere.”**  
📖 **Isaac Asimov**  
A profound statement on the unifying force of knowledge.

<br>

**“The physicist cannot simply surrender to the fact that the universe is. He must understand that the universe is.”**  
📖 **John Archibald Wheeler**  
An assertion of the human drive to comprehend existence.

<br>

**“What is now proved was once only imagined.”**  
📖 **William Blake   The Marriage of Heaven and Hell (1790)**  
A poetic nod to imagination shaping reality.

<br>

**“Everything we call real is made of things that cannot be regarded as real.”**  
📖 **Niels Bohr**  
A quantum paradox at the heart of reality.

<br>

**“Between the wish and the thing, the world lies waiting.”**  
📖 **Cormac McCarthy   All the Pretty Horses (1992)**  
A meditation on longing and inevitability.

<br>

**“We live in a world where there is more and more information, and less and less meaning.”**  
📖 **Jean Baudrillard   Simulacra and Simulation (1981)**  
A prophetic critique of modernity.

<br>

**“I am large, I contain multitudes.”**  
📖 **Walt Whitman   Leaves of Grass (1855)**  
A declaration of the vast and contradictory nature of identity.

<br>

**“The mind is its own place, and in itself can make a Heaven of Hell, a Hell of Heaven.”**  
📖 **John Milton   Paradise Lost (1667)**  
A reflection on perception and free will.

<br>

**“Freedom is the freedom to say that two plus two make four.”**  
📖 **George Orwell   1984 (1949)**  
A chilling warning about truth and power.

## Extended Reflections on Identity, Memory, and Humanity

**“A human being is a part of the whole called by us ‘universe,’ a part limited in time and space.”**  
📖 **Albert Einstein**  
A scientific and philosophical view of human existence.

<br>

**“What can be said at all can be said clearly, and what we cannot talk about we must pass over in silence.”**  
📖 **Ludwig Wittgenstein   Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus (1921)**  
A defining statement on language and logic.

<br>

**“We live in the flicker   may it last as long as the old earth keeps rolling! But darkness was here yesterday.”**  
📖 **Joseph Conrad   Heart of Darkness (1899)**  
A haunting metaphor for fragile civilization.

<br>

**“The purpose of life is to be defeated by greater and greater things.”**  
📖 **Rainer Maria Rilke**  
A poetic reflection on striving beyond limits.

<br>

**“Happiness in intelligent people is the rarest thing I know.”**  
📖 **Ernest Hemingway**  
A somber acknowledgment of awareness.

<br>

**“If the doors of perception were cleansed, everything would appear to man as it is, infinite.”**  
📖 **William Blake   The Marriage of Heaven and Hell (1790)**  
A mystical assertion on perception.

<br>

**“The world breaks everyone, and afterward, many are strong at the broken places.”**  
📖 **Ernest Hemingway   A Farewell to Arms (1929)**  
A raw observation on suffering.

<br>

**“Matter tells space how to curve. Space tells matter how to move.”**  
📖 **John Archibald Wheeler**

<br>

**“The meaning of life is that it stops.”**  
📖 **Franz Kafka**  
A stark existential truth.

<br>

**“The measure of intelligence is the ability to change.”**  
📖 **Albert Einstein**  
Adaptability as intelligence.

## Closing Reflection

These passages illuminate existence, unsettle certainty, and expand awareness.  
They emerge from myth and mathematics, poetry and physics.  
Some comfort. Others disturb. All endure.

They remind us that language   when wielded with precision and courage   can hold entire universes inside a single sentence.
